The Problem

Mental health is stereotyped, which prevents many people who suffer from a mental health problem from being able to express themselves and get out of the torment in which they are living.

My Role

UX Designer
User Research, Interaction, Visual design, Prototyping & Testing

The Solution

WeMind is a game-changing app that's revolutionizing the way we approach mental health. By providing specialized support and resources. WeMind empowers individuals to cope with life's challenges, no matter how big or small. With a hotline that connects users to trained specialists during emergencies, WeMind is committed to helping people overcome even the toughest of obstacles.

Tools

Goals

  • Create a user friendly app that helps people with mental health issues.
  • Establish and create an analysis based on the interviews to understand the user needs.
  • Implement in the design process the needs of the users based on the interviews.
  • Design an app that works, has dead ands, and interaction between buttons.

Empathize Phase

User Research

Proto-persona

Thanks to the proto-persona, it was understood who was the probable user for this new application. An average age of this proto-persona was agreed upon, we understood his pain points, what he likes, what his goals are, and we base ourselves on that to conduct interviews.

Research Plan

In order to assess the needs of our user base it was important to gather quantitative data in the form of a survey as well as qualitative data through interviews.  Our team developed a survey that was distributed via social media networks in order to reach a broad audience. At the same time, we also conducted one on one interviews with the intention to gain a better understanding of unmet needs.

Quick Survey Analysis

As the results came in, we noticed that on a scale of 1 to 5, with 1 being not at all difficult and 5 being extremely difficult to talk about mental health, 27 of 39 participants rated the level as difficult or above. That lead us to continue working with the mental health app.

Interview Insights

During the interview we were amazed at the number of people who are very afraid to go to a psychologist to ask for help for their mental health. They do not consider it as important as physical health.

Affinity Diagram

While we were analyzing the interviewees, we made an affinity chart to find out what our users have in common and thus be able to offer them an app that meets their needs.

With that important information we finally created our user-persona.

User Persona

Thanks to the interviewees, it was possible to determine who our ideal user was. With this centralized and detailed information about this person, we found out what his demands were, his pain points and what he would like to find in a mental health app.

Problem Statement

"WeMind App" is designed to provide accessible and personalized mental health support to people who are unable to express themselves or are afraid to seek help. We have observed that the current market isn't meeting the growing demand for mental health services, which is causing individuals to struggle with accessing timely and affordable mental health support when trying to manage their mental health.
How might we solve the issue of limited access to mental health support so that people can receive the help they need and successfully manage their mental health?

Ideate Phase

I like I wish I wonder

Since we already understood better how our users thought and what they were looking for, we made an ideation table creating the I like I wish and what if. This allowed us to understand as users what we were looking for in this application dedicated to mental health.

Value Proposition

The organization WeMind is developing a community based mental health app to help college students to solve the problem of procrastinating in seeking out help for their mental health by combatting the many stigmas that prevent young adults from getting the help that they desperately need.
We’re better because we believe that creating and app like WeMind will offer a safe and supportive community to finally make it easier to get the right kind of help at the right time.

We’re believable because we will allow people to have a positive impact in their personal and professional life all while helping others to do the same. In helping themselves they will also be contributing to a happier and healthier society.

The storyboard helped us create a story in our minds according to the interviews that were carried out. Many of the users said that they had fallen into alcohol or sometimes even drugs to repress what they felt.
